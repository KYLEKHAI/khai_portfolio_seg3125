How I Work

I'm currently enrolled in SEG3125, a UI/UX design course at the University of Ottawa.

The course focuses on the following: Psychological principles of human-computer interaction. Evaluation of user interfaces. Usability engineering. Task analysis, user-centered design and prototyping. Conceptual models and metaphors. Software design rationale. Design of windows, menus and commands. Voice and natural language I/O. Response time and feedback. Colour, icons and sound. Internationalization and localization. User interface architectures and A PIs. Case studies and project.

My Design Workflow

I do not have any work experience with design, but I do incorporate designs in my many projects. My process begins with rough bullet-point brainstorming. I then gather inspiration from designs I like (my past work, other people's designs) and keep screenshots and references for later. With all the planning out the way, I start implementing the rough UI on Figma or Miro to before coding it in Visual Studio Code.

Aesthetic Preferences

I'm a huge fan of having a proper aesthetic to capture an entire project. I am a fan of both modern and clean interfaces as well as visuals that differ from normal. Some of my favorite design influences come from video games, such as pixel art and low-poly PS2-style graphics. I also like having vibrant colors with neutral palettes for a little display of personality.

Here are some projects that reflect different UI design styles: screen-YAY (old-retro-style) and What The Tech (modern web style)
